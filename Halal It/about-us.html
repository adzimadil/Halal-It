Our History

We wanted to try out halal food for varies cuisines, but as we search for the cuisines we wanted to try, there was little to no halal restaurant that were up to standard and could compare with a non-halal restaurant. So we had this brillant idea to spread halal food to everyone on the internet and encouraging people to start their own halal restaurants. There was foods that we wanted to try but couldn't since it was non-halal and there wasnt a halal version of it available, that disappointed us so we started Halal It. This way people could enjoy any type of foods. We have always grown up with halal food and not many people know what halal foods are so we wanted to share it with other people.

Objectives

The main goal for us is to let people try halal food, spreading our love for halal food to others. We want people to know that they are many other ways to make things delicious so that anyone can also enjoy the food. We want to open the eyes of people to halal food, it is as delicious as normal food is but with different ingredients, may even more healthy than normal. Letting people know that halal food is not as hard as they thought it was to make, will allow more people and restaurants to try halal foods on their own menu, this will allow people to have more variety of delicious foods.

Why You Should Trust Us

As you know breaking the boundaries of non-Halal food and Muslim community as well as making it easy for you to find the best whatever you want and introduce you to the need-right-now products.

Recipe Development & Testing

Our recipes primarily use fresh, unprocessed ingredients but we also believe there is a time and a place for canned, frozen, and other prepared ingredients. We start by hand-pick the recipe and study the subtitute for the non-halal ingredients. Our recipe developers team — people with experience and knowledge will later create both the non-halal and halal version of it. Our non-Muslim team members and editor will test them both and verify if the halal version taste aunthentic and is the same as the non-halal one in order to make sure we preserve it's originality.
